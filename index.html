On your 16th birthday...

30 November 2021

Today is your day - As I wake up I now have a 16 year old son. If I close my eyes, I can still remember the moment I found out I was going to be a mom. From that moment my life changed. I remember so vividly moments throughout my pregnancy, down to the day you were born, 30 November 2005. We had to be at the hospital at 6AM so they could induce labor. The nurses began all of the preparations for your arrival. As they tried to put an IV in my arm, however, I began to pass out, so they delayed induction until 9AM. At 1:17PM the doctor handed you to me and I found the piece of me I never knew was missing. I had no idea how to be a mom, but I was certain I wanted the job. I mean, who ele is more blessed than me to have you call me "mom."

Over the last 16 years there have been plenty of times I have asked myself, "Can I do this? - Can I raise him the right way?" I think so far, your dad and I have done pretty well.

I need you to know how thankful I am that God chose me to be your mother. You are the best gift I have been blessed with.

As you move forward through life, I want you to know how proud you make me. Everyday you inspire me to be better and you give me endless hope for the future. You are a handsome, sweet, funny and loving young man and I couldn't be more proud.

Over the last 16 years I've learned so much from you, your fun-loving, silly personality, your deep oving heart for your family, your determination when you put your mind to something.

Everyday I wish I could stop time so I could enjoy you at this specific moment just a little bit longer. At the rate this is going, its just too fast for me. I want to still hold you like I used to when you still fit in my arms. I wish for those moments and relive them in my mind. I know that you are growing up and I have to start letting go, and I know that you are strong and will make a difference in this world.

Yes, I push too hard sometimes and it's not because I want to anger or frustrate you, but it's because I see so much in you and I want you to see it too. I don't want you to settle for 'ok' I wnat you to go further and reach all the limits you can.

I wish so much for you.

I wish for you to go through life safely and through all roads you travel in your future, know that the path will always be there to lead you home.

I wish for you to make good decisions, and there will be times that you don't, but that is ok, those are the ties you will learn from. I wish for you grace and strength through those times.

I wish you love - I wish for you to find someone to share your life with and laugh with. There will be broken hearts but those will make you stronger for when you find "the one".

I wish you laughter - the ability to laugh at yourself, because there will be moments in life that you simply have to laugh. That builds strength and character.

I wish for you to be a gentleman, to always treat others with respect and kindness.

I wish for you honesty - This will be hard at times but being honest is the only option. Be honest with yourself and those around you.

I wish for you trust - learn to trust yourself and stand by your ideas and beliefs. Never lose anothers trust in you, it's one of the hardest things to earn back once you lose it.

I could go on and on with my wishes for you, but most of all I wish you happiness. Happiness with yourself, you life, your family and the future.

You are my greatest "work in progress" and my greatest accomplishment. As I look through photos of the last 16 years, I wipe tears from my eyes - not of sadness but of joy of everything you've done and what the future holds for you.

I love you more than anything in this world.

I decided to put all my words out there so you could always come back to them where ever you are and whenever you need.

With love always,

Mom
